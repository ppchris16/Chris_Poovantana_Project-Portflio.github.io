Project:Electrical Compoenents Design and Integration in Chassis Strcuture

@ UBC Solar Design Team
Summer 2023

In my current role, I'm responsible for overseeing the integration of various electrical components onto the chassis CAD file. This involves not only understanding the functions of each electrical part but also strategically planning their placement while considering electrical constraints. The goal is to ensure that the electrical boards, even when exposed to challenging conditions like water and harsh weather, perform optimally and efficiently.
Part of my job includes designing and creating sturdy enclosures for all these electrical components mounted on the chassis. These enclosures need to be able to handle the vibrations generated by the vehicle and motor operations, while also be easily accessible for maintenance purporses. To keep things cost-effective, I focus on manufacturing techniques that are both straightforward and budget-friendly.

Project:Weldment Tube Notch Trimming for Production

@UBC Solar Design Team
January 2023 - April 2023

My primary responsibility centers around conducting structural analyses on the connecting tubes of our car's chassis. This involves delving into the intricate details of welding profiles that complement the chosen materials. These two pivotal aspects play a direct role in shaping the overall performance and functionality of the car chassis. It's evident that within the untrimmed section, the meshing of tubes leads to pronounced internal stress concentration at those junctions. In stark contrast, when the tubes are trimmed, this stress concentration significantly diminishes. A crucial precursor to the actual chassis manufacturing process is trimming the mesh points of the tubes. Throughout this undertaking,
I've closely followed the @VR3 production guidlines , a set of trimming regulations.
The VR3 method stands out due to its innovative approach â€“ it extracts cutting profile data straight from a 3D model. This process takes into consideration key factors like wall thickness and intersection geometry, ensuring a comprehensive 100% contact along the perimeter. This engagement underscores my proficiency in structural analysis, welding intricacies, and the art of manufacturability. It also showcases my adeptness in navigating detailed protocols like the VR3 trimming procedure. This hands-on experience and in-depth comprehension of chassis dynamics add a significant dimension to my growing project portfolio.

Project:Vehicle's Component Placement on Chassis structure

@ UBC Solar Design Team
January 2023 - April 2023

My role extends beyond the technical intricacies of design; it also encompasses the strategic orchestration of crucial vehicle components within the UBC Solar car. One of my key responsibilities involves determining the optimal location and placement of vital elements such as the Personal & Ballast Box, Motor Controller, and Steering Wheel Rack and Pinion.

This task involves a delicate balance of various factors: maximizing functionality, optimizing weight distribution, and ensuring driver comfort. It's a puzzle where every piece needs to fit perfectly for the bigger picture to work seamlessly. To achieve this, I've engaged in the creation of a mock chassis prototype. This step is paramount in guaranteeing that these components not only find their place theoretically but also practically. By integrating them into a realistic representation of the chassis, I can assess their positioning from multiple angles. This process ensures that the components fit harmoniously, don't interfere with each other, and most importantly, provide an ergonomic and comfortable experience for the driver.
